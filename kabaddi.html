Kabaddi Information

Kabaddi is a traditional Indian team sport that combines physical strength, speed, strategy, and mental toughness. It originated in ancient India and is widely played in countries such as India, Iran, Bangladesh, and South Korea. Kabaddi is played between two teams of seven players each on a rectangular court divided into two halves. This rule demonstrates breath control and ensures the raid is completed in a single attempt. Defenders, called anti-raiders, try to stop the raider by tackling them before they cross the midline. Points are awarded for successful raids and tackles, and players who are tagged or tackled are temporarily out of the game.

A standard Kabaddi match is divided into two halves of twenty minutes each, with a short break in between. Kabaddi has gained international recognition through professional leagues and global tournaments. The Pro Kabaddi League (PKL) has significantly increased the popularity of the sport by modernizing its presentation and attracting young audiences. International Kabaddi competitions are governed by organizations such as the International Kabaddi Federation, which organizes world-level championships and promotes the sport globally.

Kabaddi offers several physical and mental benefits, including improved strength, agility, endurance, reflexes, and decision-making skills. It also teaches discipline, teamwork, and courage. Kabaddi can be pursued as a professional career, with opportunities as players, coaches, referees, and sports trainers. Its cultural significance and exciting gameplay make Kabaddi one of the most respected and loved traditional sports in India.
